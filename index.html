Creme Brulee

France Dessert

Crème brûlée's history is a contested blend of French, English, and Spanish culinary traditions dating back to the 17th century. While similar custards existed earlier, the first known printed recipe for modern crème brûlée appeared in François Massialot's 1691 French cookbook, Le Cuisinier Royal et Bourgeois. The dish, meaning "burnt cream," gained global fame after being revived in the 1980s by New York's Le Cirque restaurant.

Fresh And
Natural Ingredients Recipe

Heavy Cream

500 ml
Min. 35% fat content for maximum silkiness.

Egg Yolks

5-6 Large
Only use the egg yolks; that's the key to a silky smooth texture.
Extra sugar for the torching.

Caster Sugar

50g
Whisk into egg yolks for the custard base.

Vanilla Bean

1/2 Pod
Or 5ml high-quality vanilla bean paste.

Salt

A little bit
Balancing the sweetness and enhancing flavor.

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 150°C. Heat cream with vanilla until just simmering.
  2. Whisk egg yolks with sugar until pale and slightly thickened.
  3. Slowly pour the warm cream into the yolk mixture, stirring constantly.
  4. Strain through a fine sieve, add a pinch of salt, pour into ramekins.
  5. Bake in a water bath for 40–45 min until set with a slight jiggle in the center.
  6. Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  7. Before serving, sprinkle an even layer of sugar and torch until caramelized.
